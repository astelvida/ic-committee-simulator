IC Sim

An adversarial Investment Committee simulator. Pull a deal from your live Notion pipeline or paste a brief. Defend it against four AI committee members with distinct attack vectors. Receive a five-dimension scored report. Built around your actual SSI framework. Worked example throughout: TORTUS AI (SSI 82, P0, Seed, Healthcare AI).

01 · Concept

The pitch in one paragraph

IC Sim drops you in front of four AI committee members. The Skeptic attacks market thesis. The Operator attacks execution. The Regulatory Hawk attacks compliance moat. The Portfolio Lens attacks fund fit. You defend a real deal — pulled live from your Notion Dealflow or pasted in — over eight to twelve exchanges in about fifteen minutes. A judge scores your performance against a five-dimension rubric and produces a shareable PDF report. The point is not to win. The point is to find the questions you cannot yet answer, before a real partner finds them for you.

02 · User journey

From URL to report in 18 minutes

The complete flow has five surfaces. The deal entry chooses between three input modes. Notion is the most powerful because it ties to your live pipeline; manual paste handles deals from elsewhere; sample loads TORTUS AI for first-time visitors.

03 · Four committee personas

Each persona owns a distinct attack vector

The committee disagrees on purpose. Each persona has its own system prompt, its own keyword triggers, and its own preferred lines of attack. When they conflict, the conflict is real — the Skeptic and the Regulator will give contradictory advice on the same deal, and you have to navigate both.

Persona 1
The Skeptic
Market thesis, TAM, competition, incumbents, substitution risk, why-now. Defaults to "this doesn't matter or won't work." Cites comparables and historical failures.
Heidi already does this and is free for clinicians. Walk me through who pays TORTUS and why they wouldn't standardise on Heidi inside twelve months.
Persona 2
The Operator
Unit economics, burn, hiring, runway, gross margin, ship velocity, technical debt. Has built and shipped. Will not let qualitative answers stand.
£4.2M from Khosla plus the £3.6M MHRA programme funding gives you about £7M effective runway at current burn. What's the next 12 months of hiring and how does that change the burn curve?
Persona 3
The Regulatory Hawk
EU AI Act, DORA, GDPR, MDR, MHRA, ISO/IEC frameworks. Knows the article numbers. Will question whether the compliance moat is real or theatre.
Class IIa requires post-market surveillance under MDR Article 83. TORTUS has 24 people. Walk me through who runs vigilance reporting when X-on Health doubles the install base.
Persona 4
The Portfolio Lens
Fund construction, ownership, follow-on capacity, conflict with portfolio companies, signaling risk to co-investors. The most institutional voice.
Tandem Health is at SSI 86 in your own pipeline. Both target healthcare workflow. How does this not cannibalize Tandem if you back both?
04 · Scoring

Five dimensions, ten points each, fifty total

The judge agent (a separate Sonnet call at session end) scores the user against five dimensions. Each scored 1-10. Total maps to a verdict band. The rubric rewards proactive risk acknowledgment, specific data, and thesis discipline. It does not reward agreeing with the committee — strong defenses can disagree, as long as the reasoning holds.

Conviction clarity
How clearly the user states their thesis on the deal and holds it under pressure
"Strong: names the catalyst in turn 1, restates after pushback." "Weak: hedges, retreats to vague claims when challenged."
/ 10
Risk acknowledgment
Whether the user surfaces material risks before being asked
"Strong: names top 3 risks in opening defense." "Weak: only acknowledges risks the committee raises."
/ 10
Data density
Specific numbers, dates, named comparables, cited sources
"Strong: 4 incident reports, median 6 days to file, vs MDR 15-day requirement." "Weak: 'we're growing fast.'"
/ 10
Thesis alignment
How tightly the defense ties to a coherent investment thesis
"Strong: deal explicitly mapped to Vertical SoR thesis with named catalysts." "Weak: thesis-shopping, retrofitting."
/ 10
Poise under pressure
Composure when challenged, willingness to disagree, clean correction of partial answers
"Strong: corrects the Regulator's incomplete summary cleanly." "Weak: capitulates or gets defensive."
/ 10
Five-dimension rubric · 50 points total · verdict thresholds 42+/35+/28+/below
05 · System architecture

How the pieces fit together

Stateless Next.js server handling streaming responses via Server-Sent Events. Notion MCP for live deal data. Anthropic API for the four agents plus the orchestrator's evasion classifier plus the judge. Vercel KV caches generated briefs and (Phase 2) session history. Mobile-first responsive throughout.

06 · Notion integration

Your live Dealflow becomes the deal selector

When the user clicks Pull from Notion, the app queries the Dealflow data source at session start. Filters mirror the existing Notion filters: thesis, stage, priority, signal tier. SSI score is shown as a colored badge — vermillion for highest-conviction, amber for strong, gray for emerging. Direct MCP read on every session, not nightly sync — the pipeline is live thinking, and staleness defeats the purpose.

07 · Detailed brief view

The pre-IC reading room

Once a deal is selected, the user gets 2-3 minutes with the full brief before entering the room. Every relevant field from the Notion row is surfaced: SSI breakdown, key signal, last raise, catalyst window, anti-thesis filter, regulatory exposure. The brief stays accessible in the IC room as a left rail — but the user reads it here first. Web search enrichment (see next section) appends current data the Notion row doesn't have.

Why this is on the table

TORTUS sits at the intersection of two thesis pressures: deep workflow embeddedness in the EHR (write-loop ownership, not overlay) and a regulatory moat that gates competitors. The MHRA registration as a Class IIa medical device is the kind of certification that takes 18 months and €400K to replicate. Once 3,500 GP practices are routed through it via X-on Health, switching cost is institutional, not technical.

30-day signal · from Notion

MHRA AI Airlock Phase 2 inclusion + £3.6M programme funding (Apr 2026). GOSH multi-site rollout expanding. The catalyst — 91-day window is the time before next round.

Web enrichment · current as of session

Heidi raised $19M Series A in March 2026 from Sequoia. Nuance Dragon Ambient eXperience priced at $200/clinician/month. Epic announced "Smart Prompts" feature in their April 2026 product roadmap — limited beta, no GA date. UK NHS Long Term Workforce Plan released May 2026 with explicit AI documentation budget line of £80M FY2026.

The defense you are giving

You are recommending TORTUS as a seed-stage check at a £15M post-money cap. The committee will not give you "is this a good company" — they will ask:

— Why would a hospital that already uses Heidi or Nuance switch?
— What protects you from being a feature in Epic / Cerner / Doctolib?
— Why £15M post when the last round was at $25M post 18 months ago?
— What's the GP-vs-hospital go-to-market sequence?

Kill criteria · from Notion

If Epic ships native AI documentation in next 9 months → conviction drops 30 points. If MHRA Airlock pulls back to Phase 1 → 15 points. If GOSH expansion stalls → 10 points.

08 · Web search enrichment

How agents reference current data without per-turn search latency

Agents need current data. The Skeptic citing Heidi's 2024 pricing is embarrassing in a 2026 session. But mid-turn web search adds 2-3 seconds to every question and is hard to predict which turns need it. The answer is pre-session enrichment: a fixed set of web searches runs once when the deal is selected, the results are appended to the brief, and the brief becomes context for every agent's system prompt.

The five enrichment queries

When a deal is loaded, five parallel web_search calls run with the company name, stage, sector, and known catalysts:

Figure · Pre-session enrichment · 5 parallel searches · ~2-3s total · cached 24h

Why pre-session, not mid-turn

Mid-turn search forces every potentially-searching turn to pay 2-3 seconds of latency. Pre-session pays the latency once (parallelised, ~3s total) during the brief view, which the user reads anyway. Cached results live for 24 hours keyed by deal ID + day, so re-runs of the same session within a day are instant.

Phase 2 exception: targeted fact-check

If the user makes a specific factual claim during a turn — "Heidi just raised $50M" — the orchestrator triggers a single fast Haiku-driven fact-check call before the next agent speaks. The next agent then has the verified or contradicted claim in its context. One injection point per session at most, not per turn.

09 · Turn orchestrator

How the orchestrator decides who speaks

A naive round-robin makes agents feel scripted. A pure-LLM "pick the next speaker" call wastes tokens and adds latency. The right answer is a rule-based decision tree with one small LLM call for response quality assessment.

Figure · Turn orchestrator · four-check decision tree · evasion detection first

Session end logic

Three triggers, evaluated after each turn. Turn count ≥ 12 forces a hard end. Time ≥ 18:00 forces a time cap. Turn count ≥ 8 with all four personas having spoken at least once and elapsed ≥ 15:00 triggers a soft end. User can manually click "I'm done" at any time.

10 · IC Room states

Live committee view · two display modes

The IC Room is the screen the user spends most of their time on. Card view shows live committee state — active speaker highlighted, queued members preview what they're considering. Transcript view shows the full conversation as a chat log. User toggles between them; both render from the same turn array.

11 · Voice mode

Phase 2 input · Phase 3 full voice

Voice is not MVP. Text round-trip is ~3s per turn; voice round-trip is 5-8s. That breaks the pressure dynamic. Phase 2 adds push-to-talk input (Whisper, fast, cheap) while keeping agent responses as text. Phase 3 adds ElevenLabs TTS for paid tier — four distinct voices, gated to €15/month subscribers.

Pause and timeout

Two states the user will encounter. Pause/exit modal triggers on close-button click — session state is in-memory in MVP, so leaving means losing the session. Timeout warning appears at 16 minutes with a soft option to wrap up; the rubric rewards proactive risk surfacing in the closing turn.

14 · Mobile breakpoints

Voice mode is more usable on mobile than desktop — tap-and-hold the circle.

15 · Triggering surfaces

Three ways IC Sim is triggered

The same engine serves three surfaces, each with a different purpose. The web product is the public artifact and the canonical version. The personal skill is the private rehearsal mode running inline in Claude chat. The shared link surface is what propagates the product organically.

Surface 1
Public web product
The canonical experience. Mobile-first, polished UI, full agent committee, scored PDF. What gets shared on LinkedIn and Substack. What investors and candidates see when they hear about it.
→ ic-sim.anefi.vc
→ anefi.vc home CTA
→ Substack essay embed
→ LinkedIn launch post
Surface 2
Personal skill (private)
Runs inline in your Claude chat alongside deal-memo, market-map, signal-sourcing. No UI. Pulls deal from Notion, conducts the session as conversation, produces the scored output as a Notion page. For your own prep before real interviews.
→ /ic-sim TORTUS AI
→ "run an IC simulation"
→ "practice defending [deal]"
→ Notion DB write-back
Surface 3
Shared result links
When someone shares an IC Sim result, the URL takes the viewer to the scored report with a clear "Run your own" CTA. This is the viral loop: every shared result is a recruitment touchpoint for new users.
→ /r/[slug] result page
→ OG image preview
→ "Try with your own deal"
→ Score badge embed

Phase 2 unification

In Phase 2, the skill gains a share flag: /ic-sim TORTUS share runs the simulation inline, scores it, and generates a public-readable URL on the web product with the transcript pre-populated. One command, both surfaces.

16 · Build roadmap

Two-week MVP, then iterate

Week 1

Foundation + Notion + brief

Next.js 15 scaffolding, Tailwind v4, design system. Four agent system prompts written and tested manually against TORTUS. Notion MCP integration: OAuth, dealflow query, page fetch, brief generator LLM call, caching layer. Web search enrichment endpoint. Detailed brief view UI. IC room UI scaffolding (desktop + mobile).

Week 2

Orchestrator + judge + ship

Orchestrator logic with evasion detection (Haiku call), silence detection, keyword triggers, round-robin. Streaming API endpoint (SSE), token-by-token rendering. Judge agent with rubric scoring. Post-IC report UI, PDF export. Mobile polish. End-to-end testing on TORTUS, Deeploy, Numalis, Alinia, Noteless. Deploy to ic-sim.anefi.vc.

Week 3

Launch + iterate

Public launch: Substack essay, LinkedIn post, X thread, OG image with TORTUS sample run. Direct outreach to MBA programs, analyst contacts at target funds. Iterate on user feedback. Watch for: completion rate, score distribution, share rate.

Week 4 +

Phase 2 features

Voice input (Whisper). Session history (Vercel KV opt-in). Public shareable URLs. Fund-specific IC styles. Personal skill integration with /share flag. Targeted fact-check mid-turn for specific claims.
